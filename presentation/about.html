Welcome to Unity Volunteers Trust

Our Goal Is to Make a Difference Together

Unity Volunteers Trust, established in 2023 and located in Seeduwa, is dedicated to empowering communities through volunteer action. We organize events such as beach cleanups, blood donation drives, and awareness campaigns, bringing people together to create positive social impact. Our mission is to connect passionate volunteers with meaningful causes, inspire collective action, and make a lasting difference in the lives of those in need.

Through our efforts, we aim to help people gain the skills and confidence to improve their lives and communities. Every action, big or small, contributes to lasting change. Join Us today and be part of a movement that inspires hope and transforms lives.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create empowered and self-reliant communities where individuals have the skills, confidence, and opportunities to shape their own futures. By promoting volunteering and continuous skill development, we aim to reduce poverty, support education, protect the environment, and strengthen social connections. Through collective effort and compassion, we strive to bring lasting positive change that uplifts lives and inspires a spirit of unity across all communities we serve.

Our Mission

Our mission is to connect caring volunteers with meaningful causes, support community programs, and provide opportunities for people to learn new skills, grow in confidence, and build a better future together.

How We Support You

We know that choosing to volunteer is a big step, and we’re here to guide you every step of the way.

Our team has years of experience working with volunteers in different communities. We make sure every volunteer feels prepared, confident, and valued throughout their journey.

When you volunteer with us, you’ll receive full support — from training and skill-building sessions to on-site guidance — so you can focus on making a real difference.

Your safety and wellbeing are always our top priorities. We ensure you’re well-informed, medically fit, and supported by our local coordinators during your volunteer work.
